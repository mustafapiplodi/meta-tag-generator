SEO Best Practices & Tips

Learn how to optimize your meta tags for better search rankings

Meta tags help search engines understand your content and display it attractively in search results. A well-written title and description can significantly increase your click-through rate (CTR), which is a ranking factor. They're your first impression to potential visitors.
Keep it under 60 characters, include your primary keyword near the beginning, make it compelling and unique for each page, include your brand name at the end, and accurately describe the page content. Avoid keyword stuffing and clickbait.
Write 140-160 characters, include a clear call-to-action, highlight unique benefits, use active voice, match search intent, include relevant keywords naturally, and make it enticing enough to earn clicks. Think of it as ad copy for organic search.
Open Graph (OG) tags control how your content appears when shared on social media platforms like Facebook, LinkedIn, and WhatsApp. Without them, these platforms may show incorrect or unattractive previews, reducing social engagement and traffic. The image is especially crucial - posts with images get 2.3x more engagement.
Use 1200x630 pixels (1.91:1 aspect ratio) for universal compatibility across Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn. This size prevents cropping and ensures your image displays properly. Keep file size under 300KB for fast loading. Use JPG for photos and PNG for graphics with text.
Use official validation tools: Facebook Sharing Debugger (shows how your content appears on Facebook), Twitter Card Validator (previews Twitter cards), and Google Rich Results Test (validates structured data). Always test before launching to catch errors early.
Validate Your Meta Tags

After adding meta tags to your website, test them with these official validation tools:

  • Facebook Sharing Debugger - Test Open Graph tags and preview Facebook shares
  • Twitter Card Validator - Validate Twitter Cards and see preview
  • Google Rich Results Test - Check structured data implementation

What are Meta Tags?

Meta tags are HTML elements that provide metadata about your web page. They don't appear on the page itself but in the page's code. Search engines like Google use meta tags to understand your content and display it in search results. Social media platforms use Open Graph and Twitter Card tags to create rich previews when your content is shared.

Properly optimized meta tags can improve your search engine rankings, increase click-through rates from search results, enhance social media engagement, and drive more traffic to your website.
